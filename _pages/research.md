<h2 style="margin-top:0;">Scientific Machine Learning</h2>

<img src="/images/20260327_Vehicle.png" alt="Vehicle Collision Dynamics" style="width:100%; border-radius:6px;" />

<p>
This study presents a <strong>rigid-deformation decomposition AI framework</strong> for 3D spatio-temporal prediction of vehicle collision dynamics. Conventional implicit neural representations struggle to capture high-frequency deformation modes in crash simulations. We address this by decoupling global rigid-body motion from local structural deformation through two scale-specific networks (<strong>RigidNet</strong> and <strong>DeformationNet</strong>), achieving a 29.8% reduction in rigid-body motion error, a 17.2% reduction in total interpolation error, and a 46.6% reduction in angular extrapolation error compared to undecomposed baselines. The framework recovers 92% of the directional correlation and 96% of the spatial deformation localization accuracy relative to an oracle upper bound, enabling physically consistent predictions for nonlinear collision dynamics.
</p>

<ul>

<h2 style="margin-top:0;">Optimization Algorithm</h2>

<img src="/images/2025_NEUCOM_graphical_absract.png" alt="PVTTCG Graphical Abstract" style="width:100%; border-radius:6px;" />

<p>
This study introduces the <strong>Projected Variable Three-Term Conjugate Gradient (PVTTCG)</strong> algorithm, designed to overcome the trade-off between fast convergence and strong generalization in deep neural network training.<br/>
By stabilizing the optimization path through geometric projection, PVTTCG consistently improves generalization performance across benchmarks and real-world applications such as vehicle crash prediction.
</p>

<strong>Reference:</strong>
<ul>
<li><strong>Kim S</strong>, Kim H*, Kang N, Lee TH* (2025) Projected variable three-term conjugate gradient algorithm for enhancing generalization performance in deep neural network training. <a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/neurocomputing"><em>Neurocomputing</em></a>, 131568. <a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neucom.2025.131568">https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neucom.2025.131568</a></li>
